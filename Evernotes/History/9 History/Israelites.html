Hebrew monotheism
-Unlike earlier forms of monotheism in other cultures, Israelite monotheism became the religion of the entire people and required the Israelites to disavow all other gods.
-Earlier forms of monotheism from other societies included Henotheism - the belief and worship in one god while also acknowledging the existence of other gods.

Monotheism
Unlike earlier Mesopotamian accounts of deities, which suggested gods were arbitrary in their treatment of humans, Israelite accounts noted that YHWH offered protection and favorable treatment if humans oveyed commandments.
