Background: What happened to the Israelites?
In 589 BC, the Babylonian King Nebuchadnezzar II laid siege to Jerusalem. In 587, the temple was destroyed and Nebuchadnezzar II exiled the Israelites to Babylon.

Cyrus the Great (559-530 BC)
-Cyrus consolidated the Persian tribes under his rule.
-After throwing off the reigns of the ruling Medes, the Persian Empire expanded from the Persian Gulf to the Halys River.
-As the Persian Empire reached the frontiers of Lydia, Lydia;s king Croesus became leery of the Persians.

Croesus' Preemptive War
-Croesus consulted the Oracle of Delphi, who mentioned that he would "destroy a great nation."
-Thus, in order to prevent Persia from growing too strong, King Croesus decided to launch a preemptive attack against Cyrus in 546 BC.

Fall of Lydia
-Cyrus handed a stunning defeat to Croesus, thus incorporating Lydia into the Persian Empire
-Cyrus then invaded Mesopotamia in 539 BC and quickly captured Babylon

Freeing the Jews in Babylon
-After conquering Babylon, Cyrus freed the Jews held captive there following Nebuchadnezzar's defeat of Jerusalem in 597 BC
-Cyrus allowed the Jews of Babylon to return to Jerusalem and gain a degree of autonomy
-However, Israel was a client state of the Persians
Client State- a state that is economically, pollitically, or militarily subordinate to another more powerful state. Persia made the leaders of client states subservient to Persian leaders.

Cyrus' Legacy

-Cyrus was killed in a battle in 530 BC, but had made Persia the largest empire in the world.
-The Cyrus Cylinder, excavated from Babylon, details Cyrus' conquest of Babylon and matches up with the biblical account of the same event.
-Cyrus' conquest of Babylon was used as a metaphor for the Apocalypse in the Book of Revelation in the Bible.
-Cyrus went down in history as a liberator and a benevolent dictator.

Religion in Persia
-Zoroastrianism was practiced widely in the Persian Empire.
-Along with Buddhism and Judaism, it was the third most widely practiced religion in the world before Christianity and Islam.

Who was Zoroaster?
-A Persian prophet who wished to rid the Persian tribes of polytheism, sacrifice, and sorcery.
-He taught that there was only one god, called Ahura Mazda, translated to "the Wise Lord."
-According to Zoraster, Ahura Mazda represented truth and light.
-Ahriman was the god that represented evil and darkness.
