Zhou Dynasty
  • Longest-lasting dynasty in Chinese history
  • Followed the Shang Dynasty - The Shang considered the Zhou “barbarians”
            
            King Wu, Zhou
  • First king of the Zhou Dynasty
  • Reigned from 1046 - 1043 BC
  • House of Shang was feuding internally; Wu used this to his advantage by attacking during this time - Battle of Muye

            Expanding Territory
  • The Zhou expanded territory greatly
  • This expansion, however, weakened the state(hard to govern all of the land)
  • The Zhou implemented a system of fengjan, much like feudal Europe

  fengjian: system in which king exchanges land and power for loyalty

            Zhou Ruling Style
  • Ruling class was mainly united by kinship ties.
  • Family relations were arranged by marriage where no kinship links existed.
  • Local lords were expected to accept the authority of the king as the head of a large family.
  • This ruling style was essentially decentralized.
  • Zhou kings handed large swathes of land to warriors and relatives.

            Western / Eastern Zhou
  • The Zhou is divided into the Western Zhou (1050-771 BC), when the capital was near Xian, and the Eastern Zhou (770-221 BC), when the capital was moved eastward to Luoyang.

            Chinese Political Thought
  • After the displacement of the Western Zhou and the movement of the Zhou capital eastward, China was divided into a number of small states competing for power
  • Many philosophical schools of thought emerged during this period of political and social turmoil known as the “Warring States Period” : 480 - 221 BC
  • Several of these philosophic schools have had lasting impacts on Chinese civilization and political order, among them, Confucianism, Daoism, and Legalism.

            Confucius(551-479 BC)
  • Little is known about Confucius’ life
  • “Advance the wise and the upright. set aside the crooked”
  • He was a government worker
  • He lived in exile
  • He is best known for his book, Analects, which is comprised aphorisms. 
  • aphorisms- a pithy observation that contains a general truth
  • Confucius wrote widely about filial piety
  • filial piety- a virtue of respect for one’s parents, elders, and ancesters.

            Qin(Ch’in) Dynasty
  • Followed the Zhou Dynasty, after the Warring States Period
  • Remember: During the Zhou, China was not unified, but had a fuedal-like system.
  • Qin developed an alliance with the Zhou through marriage relations
  • King Ping of Zhou(770 - 720 BC) transfered titles of the nobility and huge estates to the chief of Qin.

            Qin during Warring States Period
  • During the Warring States Period, the states of Qin and Chu were the strongest due to the locations of these two states being able to command vasy resources.

            Strength of the Qin
  • What finally gave them victory over the other states was their ruthlessness in battle.
  • Shang Yang (356-338 BC), a Qin statesman advocated total war
  • Ying Zheng, King of Qin, who emerged victorious from the Warring States period and proclaimed himself Shi Huangti - ‘first emperor’ - of China in 221 BC. About 230 BC.
