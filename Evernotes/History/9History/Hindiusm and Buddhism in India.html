Hinduism
  • Hindiusm was present in India during the rule of the Maurya, with Buddhism emerging in approximately the 5th century BC
  • Hindiusm has no single founder
  • The term ‘Hindu’ was derived from the river or river complex of the northwest, the Sindhu
  • Most Hindus revere a body of texts as sacred scripture known as the Vedas, and most Hindus draw on a common system of values known as dharma.
  • Hindiusm is a pantheistic religion (That the entire universe is a manifestation of God.)

Vedas - Ancient Hindu scriptures that hymns, philosophy, and discussion of ritual. They are written in Sanskrit

Vedas
The Vedas are made up of four compositions, and each veda in turn has four parts which are arranged chronologically.

The Samhitas are the most ancient part of the Vedas, consisting of hymns of praise to God.

The Brahmanas are rituals and prayers to guide the priests in their duties.

The Aranyakas concern worship and meditation.

The Upanishads consist of the mystical and philosophical teachings of Hinduism.


dharma - duties, behaviors, and virtues that are required to be in accordance with the cosmic order. 

Bhagavad Gita
Part of a Hindu Sanskrit epic, it is the dialogue between the warrior Arjuna (before a great battle), and his chariot driver, Krishna, who is actually the Hindu god Vishnu

Caste System 
Many believe that the groups originated from Brahma, the Hindu God of creation.
Brahmins - priests and teachers
Kshatriyas - warriors and rulers
Vaishyas3j - farmers, traders, and merchants
Shudras - labourers
Dalits(outcastes) - street sweepers, latrine cleaners

Manusmriti: widely regarded to be the most important book on Hindu law and dating back to at least 1,000 years before Christ was born, “acknowledges and justifies the caste system as the basis of order and regularity of society.”

Four Noble Truths
There is suffering
There is a cause of suffering
There is an end to sufferings
There is a path to the end of suffering

Eightfold Path 
Right View: To view the world the right way and possess observations that lead away from delusion

Right Thought: Eliminate greed and ignorance by maintaining one’s mind in accordance with the dharma.

Right Speech: Wholesome words and speech whic seeks to embrace truth and honesty and is not deceitful

Right Action: Behavior in accord with the truth: do not steal, do not murder. This is passive behavior. 
However, must go one step forward: seek to protect life and to be charitable.

Right Livelihood: Occupation must be a moral profession: do not do things that harm others and ourselves, and which corrupts morality

Right Effort: Do much to prevent unwholesome behavior while promoting wholesome behavior
