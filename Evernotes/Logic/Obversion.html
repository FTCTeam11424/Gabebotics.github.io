Obversion is a second operation we can do with logical propositions Principle - two negatives make a positive (double negative) 

Example: 
    Dogs are furry
    All {dogs} are {twi furry} 
Negate the copula: All {dogs} are not {twi furry} 
Negate the predicate: All {dogs} are not {twi not furry}
Becomes: No dogs are {twi not furry} 

Table of Valid Obversions: 
  1. A becomes E
  2. E becomes A
  3. I becomes O 
  4. O becomes I 

Examples: 
    “All men are mortal” becomes “No men are immortal”
    “No lakes are oceans” becomes “All lakes are non-oceans”
    “Every genius is a non-conformist becomes “No genius is a conformist”
