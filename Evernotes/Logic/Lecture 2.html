Classifying Terms
A proposition’s truth can be evaluated when the terms are all clear and unambiguous 
  1. Ambiguous (multiple possible usage) OR unambiguous (Only one possible usage)
    • “Dog, you are sick!” (Dog is ambiguous, does it refer to animal or human?) 
    • “My dog is healthy” (Dog is unambiguous, clearly refers to animal) 
  2. Clear (understood) or unclear (not understood) 
    • “The phaser is unstable!”
      • Background 1: This line comes from a Star Trek episode where all officers carry weapons called phasers
      • Background 2: This phrase comes from a Twilight Zone episode where there is a race of aliens called phasers have invaded earth
  3. Exact (quantitative) or vague (qualitative)
    • Exact terms are precisely measurable, and expressed as such
    • Vague terms are not mathematically measurable
    • Example: “The four hobbits had hope they would return home one day
  4. Terms are either univocal, equivocal, or analogical
    • The distinction occurs when terms are used multiple times in either the same sentence or in closely related sentences
    • Univocal - the term has one and only one meaning
    • Equivocal - the term has two completely different, unrelated meanings
    • Analogical - the term has meanings which are different from each other but related to one another 
      • “The good man gave his good dog a good meal.” - Analogical use of “good” (Good man - good pet owner, Good dog - obediant and loyal dog, good meal - good tasting/nutritional meal) 
      • I ate two hamburgers and you ate two apples
      • In Clarksville, TN, the town has two banks; the river running through the town has two banks.” Equivocal use of “banks” and univocal use of “town” (Banks, money holding, Banks, ground to hold in water) 

  • There are five possible relationships which a predicate may have to its subject
    • Subject = subject of the sentence
    • Predicate = what is being said about the subject. 
      • Ex. Morgan has long hair 
        • Morgan: subject 
        • Long hair: Predicate
  • These relationships are called “predicables” - a predicable describes the way in which the subject and the predicate are connected to each other; the predicables differ in how close they come to describing the essence of the subject.
    • Species - states the whole essence of the subject
      • Man is a rational animal 
        • “Rational animal” is said to be the species of Man
      • A triangle is a three sided plane figure 
      • Democracy is government by the people 
  • Genus + Species difference = species (essence)
    • Genus - States the common or generic aspect of the subject (What it shares in common with the other things of the same category) 
      • Man is a rational animal
        • ”animal” is said to be the genus of man
      • A triangle is a three sided plane figure
        • ”plane figure” is said to be the genus of triangle
      • Democracy is government by the people
        • ”Government” is said to be the genus of democracy
    • Specific Difference - The aspect of the subject which distinguishes it from the other things within its category (the second part of genus)
      • Man is a rational animal
        • ”Rational” is said to be the specific difference of man
      • A triangle is a three sided plane figure
        • ”Three sided” is said to be the specific difference of triangle
    • Property - An aspect of the subject which is not the essence but “flows from” the essence (NOT accidental) 
      • “Able to speak” is a property of man the rational animal
      • ”Having three interior angles equal to two right angles” is a property of a triangle
      • ”Able to change laws by popular consent” is a property of democracy
    • Accident - Any characteristic of the subject that is not essential (Neither part of the essence or nor “flowing from” the essence) 
      • “Bald” and “Athenian” are accidents of the subject “man” 
      • “Equilateral” and “tiny” are accidents of the subject “triangle”
      • ”Modern” and “Parliamentary” are accidents of the subject “democracy”
