Opposition - The relationship between any two propositions that have the same subject and predicate but differ in quality or quantity or both. 

  1. Contradiction - A:O. E:I - The truth of one means the falsity of the other and vice versa
  2. Contrariety - A:E - Cannot both be true; both can be false
  3. Subcontrariety - I:O - Can both be true; cannot both be false
  4. Subalternation - A:I. E:O - If the universal is true, then the particular under it must also be true; if the particular is false, then the universal above it must also be false.
