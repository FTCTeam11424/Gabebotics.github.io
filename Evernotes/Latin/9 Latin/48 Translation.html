All of the streets near the ampitheater were full of people who were coming to the spectacle. From everywhere a shout and a noise; from everywhere citizens, women, and slaves. Many men remained all night outside the gates of the amphitheater. Now the hour of the spectacle was here.   
    Cornelius, when the tickets had been handed to the clerk, was led by the slaves to the room reserved for the senators. Marcus had never seen so many different men. While Marcus was looking around astonished, he suddenly saw that Titus had already sat down. He was wishing to ask his uncle when he had come, For he knew that Titus is accustomed to rising out of bed late. But, because father was there, Marcus said nothing. How massive is the amphitheater! How great was the commotion of the spectacle! Marcus was guessing how many spectators are able to be contained by the amphitheater when suddenly there was silence. All turned their eyes to the cushioned couch. 
    "Look!" Shouted Titus. "Now Caeser enters, the love and delight of the human race!"
