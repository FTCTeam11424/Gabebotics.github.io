When the day of the wedding arrived, everyone woke up early. Aurelia was encouraging Marcus and Sextus to hurry. The slave women were running here and there to prepare everything.
  Flavia and Vinia, her mother, had now been present for a long time. Soon the other friends and neighbors were arriving. Approaching, they happily saw that the door and the doorpost had been decorated with ribbon and crowns of myrtle and laurel. Having entered into the house, they were led into the atrium, where Cornelia, having put on the white tunic, wearing the orange veil, was awaiting him. A little while later the great shouting and laughing was heard. Valerius was arriving with his neighbors and friends. 
   Cornelia was standing with the bridesmaids at the altar. With the religious rites having been prepared properly, the priest went forward to sacrifice the pig. When he had inspected the inner organs, “The omen” he said, “are good.” Then the table of the wedding was signed. Vinia the bridesmaid joined the right hand of Valerius and Cornelia. Cornelia responded to Valerius asking, “What is your name?” with “Where you are Gaius, I am Gaia.” With this having been done, everyone was shouting “Good luck!.” 
    When the dinner had been prepared, all the guests reclined, and after the best dinner, the drinking party full of laughter followed. 

    It was already becoming evening. Cornelia was sticking to her mother, Valerius was pretending that he was taking her away from her mother’s hands by force. Soon that woman was led into the new house with many accompanying. Five boys were preceding who were carrying flaming torches. The rest were following singing and laughing; They were throwing nuts to the children, who were running on all sides. When they had arrived at the house, the new bride was carried over the threshold so that she would not slip.

“How lucky is Cornelia!” exclaimed Flavia.
