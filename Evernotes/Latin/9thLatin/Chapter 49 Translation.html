Once in the greatest circus the beast game was being given to the people. All of the lions were a source of wonder for the spectators, but one of them seemed the most cruel. A certain slave out of many had been led into the beast fight whose name was Androcles. And when that lion had seen him far away, suddenly he stood as if wondering and he was then approaching the man slowly and peacefully. Then, moving his tail in a kindly and pleasent manner, he licks the hands of the man with his tongue, now nearly paralyzed with fear. Androcles, with his spirit having now been recovered, watched the lion very attentively. Then, as if a mutual recognition having been made, both the man and the lion were standing there happily. 
     This so wonderful thing greatly excited the crowd. Caesar asked Androcles, having been summoned to the Imperial Couch, why that most savage lion had spared him alone.Then Androcles told a wonderful thing. 
    He says, "While living in Africa with my master having been forced to flee because of his cruely, fled into a cave. Not much later this lion came to the same cave crying and sad, lame in one foot. At first I was hiding full of terror. But the lion, when he had seen me, approached gentle and tame and showed his foot to me as if seeking help. I pulled out a huge thorn, which was sticking in his foot and now I washed the wound without great fear. Then that lion, when his foot was placed in my hand, he laid down and slept. 
    The lion and I were living in the same cave for three years feeding on the same food. Afterwards, having been captured by the soldiers, I was led back to my master who immediately condemned me to the beasts.
    The emperor, with the slave’s story having been heard, was greatly astonished. Androcles was freed with the agreement of everyone,
