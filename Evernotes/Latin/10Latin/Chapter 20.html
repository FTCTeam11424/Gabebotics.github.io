Who has not heard the name of Hercules? Now I will speak a few about the great deeds of that man.
         Luck had led Hercules, the Greek man of whom strength was extraordinary, into slavery. Eurytheus had given twelve grave labors to miserable him. But Hercules had not been conquered by fear; neither was he able to be terrified by new animals, nor by men of this kind who never lack wikedness. Hercules overcame a big lion with his hands alone; he captured a quick deer whose horns were gold and dragged it out of the place in which he had found it, to Mycenae. Then he was sent by Euristheus against Cerberus, the fierce dog, he was able to even move this dog from the gate of Pluto itself!

After these things and others had been done, Hercules was freed from labors. However what reward was given to him? Nothing. What was the fruit of his labor? Glory and memories forever in verses of poets.
