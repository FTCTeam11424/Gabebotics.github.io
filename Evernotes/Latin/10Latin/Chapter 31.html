Rome was ruled by a proud tyrant, whose son is Sextus Tarquinius. A certain night when Tarquinius was drinking wine with friends, each man began to praise his wife. Collatinus himself said that Lucretia stands before all others: “ Let us bring ourselves to my house and let us see what my wife is doing now. Then you will know how much better my Lucretia is than yours.” Everyone responded, “Let us go down!” When they had come to that house , they found faithful Lucretia was not playing, but spinning wool. Sextus, when he saw how beautiful and modest Lucretia was, he was captured by a bad love. After a few days, when Collatinus had went away, he came back. When the food had been offered, he was led into the guest bedroom. In the middle of the night he came to sleeping Lucretia: “Silence!” he said. “I am Sextus Tarquinius; I bring iron in my hands. Submit to me or I will kill you!” Although Lucretia preferred to be killed, however finally Sextus conquered her chastity. Then he went away. But Lucretia told all these things to Collatinus who swore that he would kill Sextus himself. Then Lucretia killed herself, so that she would not seem to be a bad example for other wives: “I free myself from blame, but I do not free myself from punishment,” she said while dieng.
