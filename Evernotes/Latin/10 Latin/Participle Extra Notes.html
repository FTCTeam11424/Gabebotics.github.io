Ablative Absolutes: 

Ingredients:
  • Noun in ablative 
  • Participle in Ablative

Rule of Ablative Absolutes:
  • Translate the noun first

Examples: 

Animā captā: With the soul having been captured

Animā Carolī captā: with the soul of Charles having been captured (Caroli is genitive)

Glaucide animam Carolī capturō: With Grayson about to capture the soul of Charles 

Glaucide animam Carolī capiente: With Grayson capturing the soul of Charles.
