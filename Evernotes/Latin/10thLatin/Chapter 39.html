Marcus Antonius, as if a commander arranging his troops, was putting all words in the most suitable part of his speech. He was using gestures not to express words but for the sake of illuminating his feelings. Even if his voice is naturally slightly hoarse, even this flaw was converted into a good thing. For he was having a certain tearful and suitable thing both for the purpose of making faith and moving mercy. 
  Even if I give as much praise to Antonius as I already said, nevertheless the greatest weight was in Lucius Crassus,  When elegance had been joined with seriousness
