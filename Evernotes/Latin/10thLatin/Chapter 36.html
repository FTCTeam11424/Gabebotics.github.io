I think that the students in school are being made the most stupid, because they hear or see nothing out of these things which we have in use, but (instead they hear) men full of fear begging from pirates so that they don’t throw them into chains, but (they hear) tyrants writing proclamations by which they order sons to cut off the heads of their own fathers, but (they hear) kings having been warned by oracles that they should sacrifice three virgins so that sickness does not become worse. Those who are being nourished among these things are no more able to understand than the thing which lives in the kitchen to smell good. For the teachers accomplished with light and disgraceful speeches so that the body of an orator was weakened and falling. Certainly neither Plato nor Demosthenes agreed with this kind of discipline. 

    Agamemnon did not permit for me to plead any longer: “I admit that teachers sin in this practice, but we ought to forgive them. For unless they have said these things which is pleasing to the young men, as Cicero says, ‘They will be abandoned in schools alone.’ Parents are worthy with blame, who do not want their children to learn strict principles. Now, as boys, are playing in school; as youth, they are being laughed at in the forum.”
