Indirect statements: saying, thinking, 
Main verb that has to do with something indirect, accusatives and infinitives after. Atleast one of each.

When doing perfect in an indirect statement:
Puto puerum amavisse puellam
3rd PP add -isse
Present:
Puto Cornelium videre me.
I think that Cornelius sees me.

Present-Perfect:
Puto Cornelium vidisse me.
I think that Cornelius saw me.

Changing the time of indirect statements:
(Present-Present)
Cornelius dicit se ducere Marcum
Corneliuis says that he leads Marcus

(Perfect-Present)
Cornelius dixit se ducere Marcum
Cornelius said that he was leading Marcus

(Present-Perfect)
Cornelius dicit se duxisse Marcum
Cornelius says that he led Marcus.

(Perfect-Perfect)
Cornelius dixit se duxisse Marcum
Cornelius said that he had led Marcus.

(Present-Future)
Cornelius dicit se ducturum esse Marcum
Cornelius says that he will lead Marcus.

(Perfect-Future)
Cornelius dixit se ducturum esse Marcum
Cornelius said that he would lead Marcus.
