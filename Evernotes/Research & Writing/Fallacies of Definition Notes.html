Vagueness: Use of words that are imprecise or unclear.

Ex: Capital punishment is bad.
-bad in what way? For whom?

Ambiguity: Use of a word that has multiple meanings that changes the meaning of the sentence in which it is used.
ONLY WORDS
Ex: A good life depends on the liver.
-liver like the organ or the person who is alive?

Equivocation: When a key term in an argument is used with two different definitions in dfferent parts of the argument, yet those two definitions are treated the same.

Translation: Two different definitions of the same word in the same argument yet you act like they are the same.

Ex: Philosophy teaches people to argue better, but do we really need more conflict in the world? It's not good for people to argue.

Amphiboly: Using a phrase that can be percieced two different ways
ONLY PHRASES
Note: Not the same as Ambiguity because Amphiboly can contain phrases while Ambiguity is only individual words.

Ex: Who's on first? Whos on first
